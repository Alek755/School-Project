  • Mental Health and Well-Being

    We're here to help! Mental health is just as important as our physical health when it comes to success at school, work, and in our relationships with one another. We all want our children to feel mentally well, but it’s not always easy to know what to do. As part of our ongoing board commitment to promote positive mental health, provide early intervention and reduce barriers to culturally-responsive mental health support, we are pleased to announce the Be Well Support Line for students. Be Well Support Line for students The Be Well Support Line provides students in Kindergarten to grade 12 and caregivers of students under age 14 with direct access to registered mental health professionals from social work and psychology departments. Students and caregivers can access non-crisis individual consultation, brief individual counselling and identity-specific support, and information and referrals to school and community mental health services. Our team of registered mental health professionals have experience in supporting the mental health and wellbeing of individuals and families with various backgrounds, abilities and identities. Our team is ethnically, culturally and linguistically diverse and include (but not limited to) professionals that identify as Black, 2SLGBTQ+, South Asian, Arab, Muslim and Polish. Access the support line by: Phone: (905) 890-1010 ext. 2608 Email: bewell@peelsb.com Submit an online service request: bit.ly/PDSB-BWSL



  • Make a Donation

    Are you interested in making a donation to help a Peel District School Board student, school or program? Your donation will help us further inspire success, confidence and hope in each student. You may choose to direct your donation to your school, or to the Peel Learning Foundation. To donate directly to a school, click here. Peel Learning Foundation 1 in 5 students attending school in Peel Region face barriers related to poverty that prevent them from fully participating in school. Many students come to school hungry because there’s no food at home. Others lack basic necessities like winter clothing or school supplies. Students also face difficult challenges in the home environment where a parent or guardian has lost a job or there’s an emergency situation that requires a compassionate response. The Peel Learning Foundation is a community-based, charitable organization that raises funds to enable Peel District School Board students to achieve personal excellence by providing resources that help them overcome barriers. To learn more and donate, visit www.peellearningfoundation.org. Other charities If you are looking to donate to an external charity for a school fundraiser (e.g. Terry Fox, Heart and Stroke), please check with your child's school to learn more.
